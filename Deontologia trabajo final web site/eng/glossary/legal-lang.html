Term Translation Definition Example
Abandonment Abandono The act of leaving a person, thing or place with no intention of returning. She filed for divorce on the grounds of abandonment.
Pidió el divorcio alegando abandono.
Abuse (of funds) Uso indebido (de fondos) The use of something in a way that is wrong or harmful. He abused the money he gained for charity.
Él usó indebidamente el dinero que haya ganado para organización benéfica.
Abuse (physical, psychological, etc.) Maltrato, malos tratos; abusos Unfair, cruel or violent treatment of somebody. He abused his wife for many years before she went to the police.
Él maltrató a su esposa por muchos años antes de que ella acudiera a la policía.
Affidavit Acta notarial A written statement that you swear is true, and that can be used as evidence in court. Levantaron acta notarial de lo sucedido.
They created an affidavit concerning what had happened.
Acknowledgement of receipt (law: verification) Acuse de recibo Confirmation that someone(usually an organisation) has received a document, payment or another business-related item. Yo firmé el acuse de recibo cuando me entregaron el paquete.
I signed the acknowledgement of receipt when the package was delivered.
Bankruptcy Bancarrota The state of being without enough money to pay what you owe. Unfortunately, the city's last book store went into bankruptcy last December.
Desafortunadamente, la última librería de la ciudad fue a la bancarrota el pasado mes de diciembre.
Boot camp (for criminal rehab) (el) Campamento de rehabilitación estilo militar A type of prison for young criminals where there is strict discipline. The boot camps are designed to deter young criminals from reoffending.
Los campamentos de rehabilitación estilo militar están diseñados para ayudar a los jóvenes criminales a no reincidir.
Civil action (la) Acción civil A civil action is a noncriminal lawsuit that begins with a complaint and usually involves private parties. A civil action is a noncriminal lawsuit that begins with a complaint and usually involves private parties.
Una acción civil es una demanda no penal que comienza con una denuncia y generalmente involucra a partes privadas.
Civil rights (los) Derechos civiles The rights of citizens to political and social freedom and equality. If you don't claim and defend your civil rights you risk losing them.
Si no reivindicas y defiendes tus derechos civiles, corres el riesgo de perderlos.
Claim(legal) Declaración A statement that something is true although it has not been proved and other people may not agree with or believe it. Their claim was that they had been cheated.
En su declaración dijo que les habían estafado.
Delinquent (legal);(accounting: overdue) Delincuente (person-offender; moroso/a (accounting: overdue) A person who has failed to pay money that is owed.(accounting)
It can also mean overdue payments. 		I refuse to date such a delinquent type; it could be dangerous.
No voy a salir con un tipo delincuente: podría ser peligroso.
Bill collectors kept calling because my account was delinquent.
Los cobradores me llamaban porque mi cuenta estaba morosa.
District Court Corte de distrito A district court, in the United States federal judicial system, refers to one of the 94 trial courts throughout the nation that determine facts and apply legal principles to both civil and criminal cases. The district court deals with the most common violations of law.
El corte de distrito lidia con las violaciones de la ley más comunes.
Evidence Evidencia, prueba The information that is used in court to try to prove something. The DNA tests could not be admitted as evidence.
Las pruebas de ADN podrían no ser admitidas como prueba/evidencia.
Federal Court of Appeals Corte federal de apelaciones The United States courts of appeals are the intermediate appellate courts of the United States federal judiciary. Each circuit has its own Court of Appeals that reviews cases decided in U.S. District Courts within the circuit.
Cada circuito tiene su propio corte federal de apelaciones que revisa los casos decididos en los Cortes de Distrito de EE. UU. dentro del circuito.
Felony Delito grave The act of committing a serious crime such as murder or rape; a crime of this type. Jerry couldn't get a job because he had been convicted of a felony.
Jerry no conseguía trabajo porque había sido condenado por un delito grave.
Felony murder Homicidio cometido en el curso de un delito grave The rule of felony murder is a legal doctrine in some common law jurisdictions that broadens the crime of murder. A felony murder is defined as the intentional or accidental death of a person by an active member of a violent or dangerous felony.
Un homicidio cometido en el curso de un delito grave se define como la muerte intencional o accidental de una persona por parte de un miembro activo de un delito grave violento o peligroso.
Findings (legal) Resultados de una investigación Information that is discovered as the result of research into something. The findings were enough to prove his innocence.
Los resultados de una investigación fueron suficientes para probar su inocencia.
Guilty (criminal) Culpable Having done something illegal; being responsible for something bad that has happened. The jury found her guilty on all charges.
El jurado la encontró culpable de todos los cargos.
Hearing (judicial) Audiencia An official meeting at which the facts about a crime, complaint, etc. are presented to the person or group of people who will have to decide what action to take. The attorney spoke at the hearing.
El abogado se dirigió a la audiencia.
Identity theft Robo de identidad The fraudulent practice of using another person's name and personal information in order to obtain credit, loans, etc. Mary was a victim of identity theft; someone else was using her name and date of birth illegally.
Mary fue víctima de una usurpación de identidad: alguien estuvo usando su nombre y su fecha de nacimiento de forma ilegal.
Ineligible child Niño quien no tiene derecho a beneficios Ineligible child means a child in the household who does not meet all of the eligibility criteria for or enrolled in medical assistance for the blind or disabled. Ineligible child means a child younger than 18 years of age who is not blind or disabled.
Niño quien no tiene derecho a beneficios significa un niño menor de 18 años que no es ciego ni discapacitado.
Internet Phishing Fraude electrónico (conocido en inglés como «Phishing») por internet A type of online scam that targets consumers by sending them an e-mail that appears to be from a well-known source. Phishing is prohibited by law.
El fraude electrónico es prohibido por la ley.
Judge Juez(a) A person in a court who has the authority to decide how criminals should be punished or to make legal decisions. She was the judge in the recently televised trial.
Ella fue la jueza en el juicio que televisaron recientemente.
Jury duty Servicio de jurado The obligation to act or a period of acting as a member of a jury in court. Jury duty might seem like a break from work, but really it's very boring.
El servicio de jurado puede parecer un descanso del trabajo, pero en realidad es muy aburrido.
Lack of severity Falta (o insuficiencia) de gravedad The quality of mercy or forgiveness, especially in the assignment of punishment as in a court case. Her punishment lacked severity.
Su castigo faltaba gravedad.
Lawsuit Demanda judicial A claim or complaint against somebody that a person or an organization can make in court. The company launched a lawsuit against their competitor for patent violations.
La empresa presentó una demanda judicial contra su competidor por infracción de patentes.
Lawyer Abogado, licenciado A person who is trained and qualified to advise people about the law and to represent them in court, and to write legal documents. Ben went to law school to become a lawyer.
Ben fue a la facultad de derecho para ser abogado.
Legal custody Custodia legal Legal custody gives a parent the right to make long-term decisions about the raising of a child, and key aspects of the child's welfare. Legal custody gives the parent the ability to make decisions for the child.
La custodia legal le da al padre la capacidad de tomar decisiones por el niño.
Legal guardian Tutor legal, guardián legal A legal guardian is a person who has been appointed by a court or otherwise has the legal authority to make decisions relevant to the personal and property interests of another person who is deemed incompetent, called a ward. A parent or a legal guardian needs to call to let a child out of school.
Para dejar salir a un niño de la escuela tiene que llamar uno de los padres o un tutor legal.
Missing person Persona desaparecida A missing person is a person who has disappeared and whose status as alive or dead cannot be confirmed as their location and condition are unknown. My husband hasn't been seen for three days and the police have recorded him as a missing person.
Nadie ha visto a mi esposo en estos días y la policía lo ha catalogado como persona desaparecida.
Non-attorney (representative) Representante no abogado A person who is not an attorney or lawyer. A non-attorney representative is a professional who represents people who are applying for disability benefits.
Un representante no abogado es un profesional que representa a las personas que solicitan beneficios por discapacidad.
Notify Notificar, avisar To formally or officially tell somebody about something. Matthew wrote to notify his boss of his intention to resign.
Matthew le escribió a su jefe para notificarlo de su intención de renunciar.
